Build a clean, modern, mobile-responsive one-page website for a B2B consulting business called “Route & Rate”.

Tech: Use HTML + CSS (and minimal JS if needed). No frameworks required. Keep the structure simple and easy for a beginner to edit.

Brand & audience:
– Route & Rate helps founder-led businesses in the UK who ship a lot of parcels but don’t have a big logistics department.
– We review their parcel shipping, compare different courier options, and help them renegotiate or re-tender their contracts to cut costs and improve reliability.

Overall style:
– Very clean, professional, “consultancy” vibe (think SaaS/consulting landing page)
– Plenty of white space, clear sections, simple sans-serif fonts
– Use a calm, trustworthy colour palette (e.g. deep blue + soft grey + one strong accent for buttons).

Page structure (sections in this order):

Navbar – logo text “Route & Rate”, simple right-side links that scroll to sections: “What We Do”, “How It Works”, “Who We Help”, “Contact”.

Hero section
– Headline: “Stop overpaying for parcel shipping.”
– Subheadline: “Route & Rate helps founder-led businesses in the UK cut parcel costs and improve courier reliability with simple, data-backed parcel tenders.”
– Primary CTA button: “Book a parcel review” (link with #contact anchor).
– Secondary text link: “Learn how it works” (scrolls to “How It Works”).

“What We Do” section
– Short intro sentence.
– 3 cards with icons/headings and brief copy:
a) “Review your current parcel spend” – “We analyse your invoices, contracts and service levels to see where money is leaking.”
b) “Compare courier options” – “We run a structured comparison of multiple carriers and services in plain English, not jargon.”
c) “Support renegotiations and tenders” – “We help you renegotiate with existing couriers or run a simple parcel tender if it’s worth it.”

“How It Works” section
– Three steps laid out horizontally on desktop, stacked on mobile:
Step 1: “Share your current setup” – “You send us recent invoices and a quick overview of how you ship today.”
Step 2: “We model your options” – “We benchmark your rates and build a clear like-for-like comparison across couriers.”
Step 3: “You choose, we support” – “You choose the route and rate that fits your business; we support implementation and negotiations.”

“Who We Help” section
– Short headline: “Who Route & Rate is for”
– Bullet points describing ideal clients, for example:
– Founder-led ecommerce or B2B businesses in the UK
– Spending roughly £100k–£500k per year on parcel shipping
– Using one or two main couriers (like DPD, DHL, UPS, Royal Mail)
– No in-house logistics or procurement team dedicated to parcels

“Why Work With Us” section
– 3 short bullets emphasising:
– “Independent” – we’re not a broker or carrier; we sit on your side of the table.
– “Focused on parcels” – we specialise in parcel shipping, not every mode under the sun.
– “Founder-friendly” – simple explanations, clear numbers, no procurement jargon.

Contact / CTA section
– Headline: “Book a quick parcel review”
– Short text: “Share a few details about your shipping and we’ll tell you honestly whether a proper parcel tender is worth it.”
– A simple contact form with fields: Name, Company, Email, Approximate annual parcel spend (dropdown or text), Message.
– Submit button: “Send enquiry”.
– Also show your email address as a clickable mailto link: adam@routeandrate.com.

Other requirements:
– Make the layout responsive and look good on mobile and desktop.
– Comment the HTML and CSS so a beginner can see where each section starts and ends.
– Put all CSS either in a <style> block in the HTML head or a single separate CSS file and show both in the output.
